UNIVERSITY OF SUSSEX: DPHIL PHYSICS (INFORMATICS)

Final Project:

Project Aim: Investigate the applicability of 1-D Petschek-type magnetohydrodynamic models to (CLUSTER II) in-situ multi-spacecraft observations of plasma parameters in dayside magnetopause reconnection events. Computational Modelling: Using MATLAB to prepare data before selecting and composing reconnection layer structures using genetic algorithms for high-resolution, multi-variate time series data from four satellites across multiple event case studies. Analysis: Classifying events in terms of location, layer structure, flow and energy profiles, and fitness of the model.

OCT 2005 - JUN 2011

UNIVERSITY OF SUSSEX: MSC PHYSICS

Final Project:

Project Aim: The analysis and interpretation of single satellite (AMPTE-IRM) in-situ measurements of plasma parameters during dayside magnetopause transition events in terms of magnetohydrodynamic reconnection theory. Computational Modelling: Using MATLAB to prepare and explore data to identify evidence of candidate transition events. Selected candidates underwent a combination of manual and computational analysis to confirm signatures of reconnection. Identified reconnection events were modelled and analysed as a series of case studies. Analysis: Events were classified in terms of likelihood of reconnection, type, layer structure, and energy profiles.
